ERASMUS+ VISA, RESIDENCE, AND INTERNATIONAL MOBILITY GUIDANCE

This document contains general guidance regarding visas, residence rights, immigration procedures, and official resources relevant to Erasmus+ mobility students.

Visa, residence, and immigration requirements depend on:
- citizenship,
- destination country,
- mobility duration,
- and national legislation.

Students should always verify information with:
- the host institution,
- official embassy or consulate websites,
- official government immigration portals,
- and Erasmus+ guidance resources.

--------------------------------------------------
EU STUDENT RESIDENCE RIGHTS
--------------------------------------------------

These rules apply primarily to EU citizens studying in another EU country.

RIGHT TO LIVE IN ANOTHER EU COUNTRY AS A STUDENT

An EU citizen has the right to live in another EU country for the duration of their studies if they:
- are enrolled at an approved educational institution,
- have sufficient financial resources to support themselves,
- and have comprehensive health insurance coverage.

The host country cannot require the student to have income above the level needed for basic income support.

LOSS OF RESIDENCE RIGHTS

A student may lose the right to stay in the host country if:
- they finish their studies,
- and cannot prove sufficient financial resources or employment.

RESIDENCE REGISTRATION

During the first 3 months of stay, the host country generally cannot require residence registration.

After 3 months, the host country may require the student to register residence with local authorities.

Students may need:
- proof of enrollment,
- proof of health insurance,
- declaration of sufficient financial resources.

No additional documents should normally be required.

REQUEST TO LEAVE OR DEPORTATION

Students may stay in the host country as long as they continue to meet residence conditions.

A host country may require a student to leave if residence conditions are no longer fulfilled.

Deportation is possible only in exceptional cases involving serious public policy or public security concerns.

Any deportation decision must:
- be given in writing,
- explain the reasons,
- and explain appeal rights and deadlines.

PERMANENT RESIDENCE

After 5 years of legal continuous residence in another EU country, EU citizens automatically gain permanent residence rights.

Permanent residence allows the person to remain in the country indefinitely.

Temporary absences generally do not interrupt continuous residence, including:
- absences shorter than 6 months per year,
- compulsory military service,
- one absence of up to 12 consecutive months for important reasons such as illness, pregnancy, work, or training.

Permanent residence rights may be lost after more than 2 consecutive years outside the country.

--------------------------------------------------
NON-EU STUDENT VISA AND RESIDENCE GUIDANCE
--------------------------------------------------

These guidelines apply to students who are not citizens of the EU/EEA or Switzerland.

GENERAL RULES

Non-EU students participating in Erasmus+ mobility may require:
- a student visa,
- temporary residence permit,
- proof of enrollment,
- proof of accommodation,
- proof of financial means,
- and health insurance.

Requirements depend on:
- the student’s citizenship,
- the host country,
- duration of stay,
- and local immigration legislation.

APPLICATION TIMING

Visa and residence procedures may take several weeks or months.

Students are strongly advised to:
- begin preparations early,
- follow instructions from the host institution,
- and consult the relevant embassy or consulate.

COMMON REQUIRED DOCUMENTS

Host countries may require:
- valid passport,
- Letter of Acceptance,
- Learning Agreement,
- proof of accommodation,
- proof of sufficient financial resources,
- proof of health insurance,
- passport photos,
- residence registration forms.

HEALTH INSURANCE

Health insurance is usually mandatory for Erasmus+ mobility.

EU citizens may use the European Health Insurance Card (EHIC), depending on the country.

Non-EU students may need private health insurance or local insurance coverage.

RESIDENCE REGISTRATION

Some countries require students to:
- register temporary residence after arrival,
- apply for a residence permit,
- or report their stay to local authorities.

Deadlines and procedures vary by country.

IMPORTANT NOTICE
--------------------------------------------------

Visa, residence, and immigration rules may change frequently and may differ between individual situations.

The university assistant provides general informational guidance only and is not a substitute for official legal or immigration advice.

Students should always:
- verify requirements with official authorities,
- follow instructions from the host institution,
- and consult the relevant embassy or consulate before travel.
