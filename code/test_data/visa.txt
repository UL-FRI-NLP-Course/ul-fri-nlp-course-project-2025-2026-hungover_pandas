Visa and Residence Permit Information for International Students at UL FRI

EU/EEA and Swiss citizens do not need a visa or residence permit to study in Slovenia. They only need a valid ID card or passport.

Non-EU students who plan to study in Slovenia for more than 90 days must apply for a long-stay student visa (type D) at the Slovenian embassy or consulate in their home country BEFORE arriving in Slovenia.

After arriving in Slovenia, non-EU students must register their temporary residence at the local administrative unit (upravna enota) within 3 days of arrival. You will need: proof of enrolment from UL FRI, proof of accommodation, valid passport, and proof of sufficient financial means (approximately 560 EUR per month).

The student residence permit is typically issued for the duration of the study programme. It must be renewed annually.

Applications for the visa should be submitted at least 2-3 months before the intended start of studies, as processing times vary by country.

For detailed and up-to-date information, consult the Slovenian Embassy in your country or the UL International Office.
