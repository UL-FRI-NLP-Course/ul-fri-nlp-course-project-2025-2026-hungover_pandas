ERASMUS+ STUDY EXCHANGE 2026/27 — FINANCIAL SUPPORT, LEARNING AGREEMENT, AND MOBILITY RULES

This document summarizes key information for UL students selected for an Erasmus+ study exchange in the academic year 2026/27. It is intended as RAG knowledge-base material for answering student questions about the steps after selection, host institution applications, Erasmus+ financial support, Online Learning Agreement (OLA), travel support, and mobility requirements.

1. AFTER SELECTION FOR ERASMUS+ EXCHANGE

After a student is selected for an Erasmus+ study exchange, the home faculty or academy nominates the student to the partner institution abroad. Some students may be nominated immediately, while others may be nominated later.

The host institution should provide the student with application instructions, deadlines, forms, and practical information. Students should also check the host institution’s website themselves, especially pages for incoming exchange students or Erasmus+ students.

Students must carefully follow the host institution’s instructions and submit all required documents by the host institution’s deadline. Many institutions use online application systems. Late applications are often not considered.

2. APPLICATION TO THE HOST INSTITUTION

The host institution application usually includes:

- Application Form: personal information and exchange details.
- Learning Agreement (LA): planned courses or academic obligations abroad and the courses or obligations that will be recognized at the home faculty after return.
- Accommodation Form or housing information, if provided by the host institution.

The Learning Agreement must usually be approved by the student, the departmental or faculty Erasmus+ coordinator, and the host institution coordinator. In some cases, the institutional coordinator may also need to sign.

The Learning Agreement may be changed later if necessary, for example if a course is not available, overlaps with another course, or is not offered in the expected language. Such changes are usually arranged within the first month after the start of the exchange.

3. ONLINE LEARNING AGREEMENT (OLA)

Students should complete the Learning Agreement online through the Erasmus+ financial support application, if the host institution supports the Online Learning Agreement (OLA).

Students must select the correct host institution in the online application so that the OLA is sent to the correct address abroad.

If the host institution does not support digital signing of the OLA, the student may use a Word/PDF Learning Agreement form instead.

4. ECTS REQUIREMENTS

For one semester of Erasmus+ study exchange, the student must complete at least 20 ECTS credits abroad and have them recognized at the home institution.

For a full academic year exchange, the student must complete at least 40 ECTS credits abroad and have them recognized at the home institution.

If a student goes abroad mainly to prepare a final thesis, or obtains fewer than 20 ECTS credits during the exchange, Erasmus+ financial support is limited to 3 months.

5. ERASMUS+ FINANCIAL SUPPORT APPLICATION

Students selected for Erasmus+ study exchange must apply for Erasmus+ financial support through the University of Ljubljana online application:
https://studij.uni-lj.si/finpomoc/finpomoc_prva.asp

The application is also used to access or complete the Online Learning Agreement (OLA).

The online financial support application must be complete at least 10 working days before the start of mobility. This is necessary so that the Erasmus+ Grant Agreement can be prepared, signed, and processed before departure.

Final application deadlines:
- First semester or full academic year exchange: 15 October 2026.
- Second semester exchange: 15 March 2027.

In many cases, the actual deadline is earlier because the complete application must be submitted at least 10 working days before the start of mobility.

Example:
If the exchange starts on 1 September 2026, the application deadline is 15 August 2026, not 15 October 2026.

6. REQUIRED DOCUMENTS FOR FINANCIAL SUPPORT APPLICATION

Before submitting the online financial support application, students should prepare the following documents:

- Letter of Acceptance from the host institution. This may be an official acceptance letter, an email confirming acceptance, or a Learning Agreement signed by all parties.
- Learning Agreement, including planned academic obligations and ECTS credits, signed or approved by the student, the home Erasmus+ coordinator, and the host Erasmus+ coordinator.
- Copy or photo of a bank card. Payments can only be made to bank accounts opened in the Republic of Slovenia.
- Certificate of enrollment for the academic year 2026/27.
- Supporting documents for additional grant for students with fewer opportunities, if applicable.
- OLS language assessment result, if completed. OLS is not mandatory but is highly recommended.

Uploaded files should be in PDF, JPG, or another appropriate format. If a document has multiple pages, it should be saved as one file. Individual documents must not exceed 2 MB.

If the host institution coordinator cannot sign the Learning Agreement before the application deadline, the student may temporarily upload a Learning Agreement signed only by the student and home coordinator. The fully signed Learning Agreement must then be uploaded within 15 days after the start of mobility.

7. ONLINE APPLICATION ACCESS AND SUBMISSION

Students first register in the online application system. Only students selected for Erasmus+ exchange at their faculty or academy can submit the application.

Students must enter their personal data accurately. The name and surname should match official documents, including special characters. Names should be written with normal capitalization, for example “Janez Novak”, not fully uppercase or lowercase.

The system recognizes students based on their original exchange application data, including the period for which they applied. If a student later changes the semester or shortens the exchange period, they may still need to select the original period during registration and inform the responsible office so the data can be corrected manually.

After entering the required data, the system generates an access code. Students must save this code because it is needed for future access to the application.

Students may edit and complete the application multiple times by saving changes. Once they choose “save and submit”, no further changes or additions are possible.

8. ERASMUS+ GRANT AGREEMENT AND DIGITAL SIGNATURE

After the financial support application is complete and all required attachments are uploaded, the student receives the Erasmus+ Grant Agreement by email.

The agreement must normally be signed using a qualified digital signature and uploaded back into the Erasmus+ application at least 10 days before departure.

The University of Ljubljana then digitally signs the agreement. The fully signed agreement remains available in the online application.

Only in exceptional cases, if a student cannot obtain a digital signature for justified reasons, the agreement may be printed, signed by hand, and sent by post to the University of Ljubljana.

9. PAYMENT OF ERASMUS+ FINANCIAL SUPPORT

After the signed Grant Agreement is returned, the University of Ljubljana transfers the first payment within 30 days or before departure, whichever comes first.

The first payment includes:
- 80% of the basic Erasmus+ financial support.
- 80% of the additional grant for students with fewer opportunities, if applicable.
- 80% of standard travel support.

The remaining funds are paid after successful completion of the Erasmus+ exchange.

Green travel differences and additional travel days are reimbursed only after return, based on appropriate documentation and fulfillment of conditions.

10. BASIC MONTHLY ERASMUS+ GRANT AMOUNTS FOR 2026/27

The monthly Erasmus+ financial support depends on the host country group.

Group 1: high cost of living countries
Austria, Belgium, Denmark, Finland, France, Germany, Iceland, Ireland, Italy, Liechtenstein, Luxembourg, The Netherlands, Norway, Sweden.
Amount: 674 EUR per month.

Group 2: medium cost of living countries
Cyprus, Czech Republic, Estonia, Greece, Latvia, Malta, Portugal, Slovakia, Spain.
Amount: 606 EUR per month.

Group 3: low cost of living countries
Bulgaria, Croatia, Lithuania, Hungary, Poland, Romania, North Macedonia, Serbia, Turkey.
Amount: 550 EUR per month.

11. ADDITIONAL GRANT FOR STUDENTS WITH FEWER OPPORTUNITIES

Students may receive an additional grant of 250 EUR per month if they meet the eligibility criteria and upload the required supporting documents when submitting the Erasmus+ financial support application.

Later submission of supporting documents for this grant is not possible.

A student may be eligible if they:
- have the status of a student with special needs;
- have a special student status, such as categorized athlete, recognized artist or cultural worker, participant in international competitions, or parent;
- or if the student or a family member in the same household has an eligible decision issued by CSD, ZZZS, ZRSŠ, or ZPIZ;
- come from a single-parent family;
- are a parent of a child under 26 years of age;
- come from a foster family;
- have international protection in Slovenia;
- are a member of the Roma community.

If the student’s name is not listed on the supporting decision, they must attach proof that they live in the same household as the person named in the decision.

Students without Slovenian citizenship may submit equivalent official documents and official translations into Slovenian or English proving disadvantaged background according to comparable criteria.

12. TRAVEL SUPPORT

Students are entitled to travel support. The amount depends on the one-way distance between Ljubljana and the mobility destination, calculated using the EU Distance Calculator:
https://erasmus-plus.ec.europa.eu/resources-and-tools/distance-calculator

Travel support amounts:

Distance 10–99 km:
- Non-green travel: 28 EUR
- Green travel: 56 EUR

Distance 100–499 km:
- Non-green travel: 211 EUR
- Green travel: 285 EUR

Distance 500–1999 km:
- Non-green travel: 309 EUR
- Green travel: 417 EUR

Distance 2000–2999 km:
- Non-green travel: 395 EUR
- Green travel: 535 EUR

Distance 3000–3999 km:
- Non-green travel: 580 EUR
- Green travel: 785 EUR

Distance 4000–7999 km:
- Non-green travel: 1188 EUR
- Green travel: 1188 EUR

Distance 8000 km or more:
- Non-green travel: 1735 EUR
- Green travel: 1735 EUR

Journeys shorter than 500 km should generally be made using green means of transport.

For distances over 500 km, students are encouraged to combine different transport modes to make the journey more sustainable.

13. GREEN TRAVEL

Green travel means that the majority of the journey, more than 50% to and from the mobility destination, is completed using low-emission transport.

Green transport includes:
- bus
- train
- bicycle
- other shared transport, such as GoOpti

The following are not considered green transport:
- airplane
- motorcycle
- car, including carpooling
- boat

Students initially receive the amount for standard travel before mobility. The difference up to the green travel amount is granted after mobility if the student proves green travel with appropriate documents.

Green travel must be proven with:
- tickets showing date and route;
- receipts;
- declaration of honor.

Travel dates on the documents must match the mobility dates confirmed in the certificate of duration.

Students using green travel may also request funding for additional travel days if needed, up to a maximum of 6 days: up to 3 days for departure and up to 3 days for return.

14. ERASMUS+ INTERRAIL PASS

To encourage green transport, an Interrail Global Pass for Erasmus+ participants is available.

It is available in two options:
- 4 travel days within a 6-month period;
- 6 travel days within a 6-month period.

15. STUDENTS WITH SPECIAL NEEDS

Students with special needs may apply for additional funding if the 250 EUR per month additional grant is not sufficient.

The application with all attachments must be submitted at least 45 days before the start of mobility. Late applications are not considered.

Examples of additional costs that may be covered:
- additional travel costs;
- adapted local transport;
- adapted accommodation;
- assistant costs;
- healthcare services;
- special learning materials.

For early information, students may contact erika.bolcina@uni-lj.si.

16. MOBILITY DURATION AND FUNDING LIMITS

An Erasmus+ exchange must last at least 2 full months, meaning 60 days.

An Erasmus+ exchange may last up to 12 months.

Maximum financial support:
- one-semester exchange: up to 5 months or 150 days;
- two-semester exchange: up to 10 months or 300 days.

Students may stay longer, up to 12 months, or up to 24 months for integrated study programmes if they have not yet used Erasmus+ mobility at the current study level, but without financial support beyond the funding limits.

Students should enter the most accurate possible start and end dates in the online application to avoid returning excess funds.

The first day of mobility is the first day the student must be present at the host institution, such as the first lecture, welcome event, or language/intercultural course included in the Learning Agreement. It is not necessarily the day of arrival.

The last day of mobility is the final day the student must be present at the host institution, such as the last lecture or exam. It is not necessarily the day of departure.

17. PHYSICAL AND COMBINED MOBILITY

Physical mobility abroad may be combined with a virtual mobility component, up to a total duration of 12 months.

The virtual part is not funded by Erasmus+ and does not count toward the Erasmus+ status quota.

This is called combined or blended mobility.

For combined mobility, the student must spend at least 2 continuous months physically abroad to be eligible for Erasmus+ financial support and additional grants.

Both the physical and virtual components must be recorded in the Erasmus+ Grant Agreement and in the Certificate of Attendance issued by the host institution after mobility.

18. ACCOMMODATION

Some host institutions provide accommodation in student dormitories. Others assist students in finding housing, while in some cities students must find accommodation on their own.

Arranging accommodation is the responsibility of each student. The University of Ljubljana is not responsible for housing issues.

Students should be cautious when searching for accommodation because online scams are common. Students should not transfer money in advance without verified information and without properly checking the accommodation.

Possible accommodation platforms:
- HousingAnywhere: University of Ljubljana rental platform with verified landlords and a possible student booking fee discount.
- Erasmus Play: https://erasmusplay.com/en/

19. LANGUAGE SUPPORT

OLS (Online Language Support) assessment through the EU Academy portal is not mandatory but is highly recommended.

The EU Academy portal also provides free online language courses.

Some host institutions may require proof of language proficiency.

20. CANCELLATION

If a student decides not to go on Erasmus+ exchange in the academic year 2026/27, they should inform:
- the University of Ljubljana Rectorate;
- the home institution;
- the host institution.

Responsible contact for cancellation at UL Rectorate:
erika.bolcina@uni-lj.si
