EXCHANGE STUDENT COURSE SELECTION

Incoming exchange students at NTNU can choose courses depending on campus, academic level, prerequisites, and course availability.

NTNU emphasizes that exchange students must be able to find enough eligible courses in the chosen city/campus. Students may only choose courses in one city.

RELEVANT CAMPUSES AND STUDY AREAS

NTNU offers exchange studies in several cities, including:
- Trondheim,
- Gjøvik,
- Ålesund.

For Computer Science and related areas, students should pay special attention to course availability in the correct campus.

The official NTNU exchange course pages list English-taught course options and refer students to programme pages for more detailed course information.

COMPUTER SCIENCE RELEVANCE

Relevant English-taught areas may include:
- Applied Computer Science,
- Information Security,
- Interaction Design,
- Software Engineering,
- Computer Science,
- and related ICT fields.

NTNU is especially relevant for students interested in:
- information security,
- applied computer science,
- interaction design,
- software systems,
- and technical computing.

IMPORTANT LIMITATIONS

Course options depend on:
- campus,
- semester,
- prerequisites,
- level of study,
- capacity,
- and whether the course is open to exchange students.

The assistant must not assume that a student can freely combine courses across NTNU campuses.

SAFE ANSWER BEHAVIOR

If a student asks about NTNU, the assistant should say that NTNU requires students to find enough eligible courses in one chosen city/campus, and that official NTNU exchange course pages should be checked before preparing the Learning Agreement.

Example answer:
“NTNU can be a good Erasmus option for Computer Science-related topics, especially applied computer science and information security. However, you must check that enough eligible courses are available in the same NTNU city/campus and confirm them in your Learning Agreement.”
