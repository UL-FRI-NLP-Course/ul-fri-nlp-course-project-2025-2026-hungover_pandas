EXCHANGE STUDENT COURSE SELECTION

Incoming exchange students at TU Graz are expected to choose courses and prepare a Learning Agreement after their exchange application has been approved.

The Learning Agreement is an agreement between:
- the student,
- the home university,
- and TU Graz.

The Learning Agreement defines which courses the student intends to take during the exchange and is used for later recognition at the home institution.

RETRIEVAL-RELEVANT NOTES FOR STUDENTS

Students should:
- check TU Graz course availability before preparing the Learning Agreement;
- use TU Graz online course systems to search for suitable courses;
- verify the teaching language of each course;
- check whether courses are offered in the correct semester;
- confirm course suitability with the home coordinator before finalizing the Learning Agreement.

COMPUTER SCIENCE RELEVANCE

TU Graz offers Computer Science-related study options and courses. The official course catalogue and English course search should be used to identify current courses in areas such as:
- computer science,
- software engineering,
- artificial intelligence,
- data science,
- information systems,
- algorithms,
- security,
- and related technical fields.

IMPORTANT LIMITATION

Course availability may change by semester. The assistant should not guarantee that a specific course will be available unless the current TU Graz course catalogue confirms it.

SAFE ANSWER BEHAVIOR

If a student asks whether a specific TU Graz course can replace a UL FRI course, the assistant should say that this must be confirmed through the Learning Agreement process and approved by the UL FRI coordinator.

Example answer:
“TU Graz has official course search systems for exchange students. You should check the current TU Graz course catalogue and English-taught course search, then include suitable courses in your Learning Agreement for approval by the UL FRI coordinator.”
